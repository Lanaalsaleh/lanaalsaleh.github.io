Reading Response 2: Glitch Studies Manifesto

date: may 13

1.The reading relates to coding because there are a lot of glitches or mistakes in coding that can transform the way a webpage looks into something unique and unusually interesting. It shows that errors are not always bad and can sometimes create creative results.

2.At first, the idea of viewing glitches or errors as creative art felt a bit confusing to understand, but as I read more, I understood how they can sometimes look visually interesting.

3.What surprised me was that some artists purposely create glitches and errors instead of trying to fix them, and use them as a form of art.

4.One of my key takeaways is that technology is not perfect, and even if people buy the latest models, imperfections and glitches can still exist. The reading explains that flaws are a normal part of technology and do not always have to be seen negatively.

5.This reading made me think more about how I have seen some artists use glitched photos as album covers for their songs, and they actually looked interesting even though I had never paid close attention to them before. It also reminded me of how some photographers intentionally choose imperfect photos because they stand out more than perfectly polished images.
